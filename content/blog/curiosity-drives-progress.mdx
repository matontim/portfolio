Science has always begun with curiosity. Before discoveries are published, technologies are built, or treatments are developed, there is usually one simple question at the center of it all: <em>Why?</em> Or perhaps more importantly: <em>What if?</em>

This website was created as a space to explore these questions in science and technology for a broad audience. Scientific discovery should not be reserved for researchers alone. Whether you are a student, professional, or simply someone who enjoys learning, there is a place for you in this conversation. 

Through my experience in both the clinical laboratory and bioinformatics, I have come to appreciate that science takes many forms. Whether analyzing gene expression data, studying disease pathways, or building computational workflows, the goal is ultimately the same: to better understand the world around us and use that understanding to improve human health and wellbeing. 

This matters because scientific progress affects all of us. Every medical breakthrough, technological innovation, and research discovery has the potential to shape our lives. We do not need to be experts in a field to appreciate these achievements, learn from them, or contribute to the broader culture of discovery. Science advances when people remain curious, ask questions, and engage with new ideas. 

I believe science should remain accessible and driven by curiosity. Some of the most meaningful discoveries begin not with certainty, but with exploration. The ability to ask questions, investigate ideas, and continuously learn is what drives both scientific progress and personal growth. 

This platform will serve as a place to share projects, research interests, and ideas related to scientific discovery in a format that is accessible to all. My hope is to create a space where learning is exciting, scientific achievements are celebrated, and where curiosity is encouraged regardless of a person’s background or expertise. Science does not discriminate, and the opportunity to learn should not be limited by barriers of education, profession, or experience. 

Progress rarely happens all at once. More often, the greatest achievements and inventions all start as a curious idea in someone’s mind. My goal is to bridge the gap between that initial spark of curiosity and the discoveries that emerge from them.
